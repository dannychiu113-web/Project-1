Facts about Lions

  • Lions are the only truly social big cats, living in family groups called prides.
  • Lionesses do about 90% of the hunting, working together in synchronized teams.
  • A lion's roar is incredibly loud and can be heard from up to 5 miles (8 km) away.
  • They are highly inactive, spending roughly 16 to 20 hours a day sleeping or resting.
  • Adult male lions are easily recognized by their thick, dark manes, which protect their necks during fights.

How to Get Involved

  1. Support wildlife conservation organizations dedicated to protecting lion habitats.
  2. Learn about the challenges wild lions face, such as poaching and habitat loss.
  3. Visit accredited, ethical zoological parks to observe lion behavior up close.
  4. Spread awareness on social media about the vulnerable status of African and Asiatic lions.
  5. Participate in citizen science projects or fundraising efforts aimed at big cat preservation.
